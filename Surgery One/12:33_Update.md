## Revised Pelvic Fracture Approach

The surgical approach for the pelvic fracture must also be modified. 
Fixation will be performed from **both the anterior and posterior sides**. 
The anterior rod will be passed subcutaneously.

**Possible complications:**

- Lateral femoral cutaneous nerve injury
- Rod-related problems (once bone union occurs, rod removal is desirable)

**Compared to the previously explained method:**

- Risk of fixation failure is reduced
- Direct wound healing issues are avoided
- Less chance of large residual displacement

**However**, in order to avoid wound healing complications from the previous surgery, a full anatomical reduction cannot be achieved.
