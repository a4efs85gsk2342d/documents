### Posterior Fixation (Prone Position Under General Anaesthesia)

- Continued from PPS; posterior pelvic ring fixation performed.
- SPF was initially planned, but CSF leak was suspected, so the sacrum was not directly exposed; closed reduction and percutaneous screw fixation was chosen instead.
- First, from the right side, a TS screw guide pin (φ3.2 mm) was advanced into S1 & S2 up to just short of the fracture site on the left.
- A Schanz pin was inserted into the left iliac crest; the left lower limb was tractioned and the pelvis reduced by pushing down with the Schanz pin; the guide pin was then advanced for provisional fixation.
- The guide pin was brought out through the left buttock, and two TS screws were inserted from the left.
- An S1 IS screw was also added for improved fixation rigidity.
- Fluoroscopy confirmed satisfactory implant positioning.
- Reduction appeared satisfactory.
- Irrigation and wound closure.

### Anterior Fixation (Supine Position)

- Position changed to supine; 3 cm skin incision at bilateral AIIS (anterior inferior iliac spine).
- Iliac screws inserted; a rod was passed subcutaneously across the abdomen for INFIX fixation.
- Irrigation and wound closure.
