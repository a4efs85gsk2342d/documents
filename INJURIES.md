## Pelvis

The **sacrum is severely fractured and comminuted** (shattered into multiple pieces rather than a clean break). You can see the fragmentation clearly in the anterior and cranial views — the central posterior “keystone” of the ring is broken apart. There also appears to be sacroiliac joint disruption on the left side, meaning the connection between the sacrum and the left hip bone has been torn apart. The entire left hemipelvis appears to have been displaced relative to the right and the spine. This is a high-energy injury pattern (typically MVA, fall from height, or significant crush).

## Spine

There’s a **sacral fracture** — the large triangular bone at the base of the spine is broken. The fracture appears to run through the sacrum (likely through or near the sacral foramina — the holes where nerves exit). Across the views you can see disruption and displacement at the sacral body, with asymmetry visible in the AP views suggesting one side has shifted. The lumbar vertebrae above look structurally intact, so this appears isolated to the sacrum/sacroiliac region.

In plain terms: the “keystone” bone that connects the spine to the pelvis is fractured. This bone bears the entire weight of the upper body and transfers it to the legs, so it’s a significant weight-bearing injury. It is classified as a **Denis Zone II fracture**. There’s visible displacement and some disruption at the lumbosacral junction. The lumbar spine above appears structurally intact.
