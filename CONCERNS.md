## URGENT — Likely Time-Critical

### 1. Left elbow fracture-dislocation has not been reduced

- Fracture-dislocations at the elbow typically require urgent closed reduction under sedation to protect neurovascular structures, especially the ulnar nerve.
- If neurovascularly compromised, this is immediate.
- **Ask:** Why has this not been reduced yet? What is the neurovascular status of the left hand (pulses, colour, sensation, motor function)? Specifically ulnar nerve function?
- **Ask:** When is reduction scheduled? Is post-reduction imaging planned?

### 2. Full body CT scan is missing

- ≥8m fall is a high-energy mechanism — CT head should be standard in a polytrauma survey, especially with unknown consciousness status at the scene.
- Calcaneal fractures, splenic injuries and diaphragmatic injuries are commonly associated with these injuries.
- **Ask:** Was CT head performed and just not uploaded? If not done — why not? Was GCS 15 throughout with no LOC?
- **Ask:** Can a full-body CT scan be conducted?

### 3. C-spine not formally cleared

- Only partially visible on lateral full-spine images. Dedicated C-spine views not confirmed available.
- High-risk mechanism of injury mandates formal C-spine clearance before collar removal.
- **Ask:** Are dedicated C-spine CT cuts available? Has a radiologist formally reported them as normal?

### 4. Paracetamol appears to be missing from the drug chart

- Foundational analgesic in WHO ladder, should be first-line in any polytrauma patient. Absence is unusual.
- **Ask:** Has paracetamol 1g QID (or Panadol Osteo 665mg SR TDS) been prescribed? If deliberately withheld — why?

### 5. Chest management — respiratory deterioration noted

- Multiple posterior left rib fractures + pain-limited breathing = atelectasis risk → secondary pneumonia.
- **New concern:** Michael has developed a croaky voice, cough, and chesty-sounding noise. Unknown whether respiratory water intake occurred during the accident (he landed in freezing water).
- This needs close attention before surgery. Antibiotics may be necessary. Staff must be made aware.
- **Action:** Get Michael using his CPAP machine. Deep breaths on the hour — this will hurt with rib fractures but lung capacity must be maximised pre-operatively.
- **Ask:** Has incentive spirometry / deep breathing exercises been commenced? Is there a chest physio plan? Has a CXR been repeated given the new respiratory symptoms? Are prophylactic antibiotics indicated?

## HIGH — Needs Clarification Within 24h

### 6. Pelvic fracture — no clear surgical plan or severity staging documented

- Complex pelvic ring disruption with sacral comminution and lateral displacement — this needs formal classification (Young-Burgess / Tile / AO).
- Unclear if delay to surgery is intentional (evidence supports 4–6 day wait for definitive fixation) or because of instability / lack of specialist availability.
- **Ask:** What is the formal injury classification? What is the surgical plan and timeline? Is the delay deliberate or logistical?

### 7. Active bleeding not ruled out

- No documented assessment for retroperitoneal haematoma or contrast CT "blush" sign.
- Pelvic fractures of this severity carry significant risk of ongoing haemorrhage.
- **Ask:** Was IV contrast CT done? Any blush sign? Current haemodynamic trend? Serial Hb trend?

### 8. L3 vertebral body — possible fracture not formally reported

- Anterior cortex of L3 looks disrupted on CT (image 3834), more subtle on MRI.
- Could be missed if attention focused on the obvious L1 crush and sacral fractures.
- **Ask:** Has the spinal team specifically commented on L3? Is there a formal radiology report covering the full spine?

### 9. Associated urethral / rectal injury not assessed or documented

- Complex pelvic fractures have a significant association with urethral disruption and rectal injury.
- Catheter placed — but blood in catheter not confirmed either way.
- **Ask:** Was the catheter draining blood? Has a retrograde urethrogram been done or considered? Rectal exam / PR performed?

### 10. Interosseous membrane disruption — left forearm

- Elbow fracture-dislocation + impacted distal radial fracture + possible distal ulnar dislocation — this pattern raises concern for Essex-Lopresti type injury / interosseous membrane disruption.
- **Ask:** Has ortho assessed the wrist and elbow injuries as a combined entity? Has forearm stability been assessed?

### 11. Bulbocavernosus reflex test — must be done before surgery

- This is a short, sharp test that determines whether Michael can defecate independently with a suppository alone, or whether he needs additional oral medication. It determines his level of bowel care required.
- **If positive (sphincter responds):** suppository will stay in and be effective.
- **If negative:** he will need additional oral medication to push stool through.
- Michael **must not go into surgery without a bowel management plan in place.** Likely needs senna (stool pusher) + coloxyl (stool softener) and possibly movicol (draws water into the bowel). A compacted bowel must be avoided.
- Last reported bowel motion was Monday (verbal report from Michael). Very little detail available — nursing staff may have more information.
- **Ask:** Has this test been completed? What was the result? What are his bowel motions like (frequency, consistency)? Has a bowel management plan been prescribed?

### 12. ASIA assessment — regular neurological monitoring required

- The ASIA (American Spinal Injury Association) test assesses both myotomes (muscle movement) and dermatomes (sensation). This is critical for detecting whether his spinal situation is worsening or stable.
- Needs to be performed regularly — possibly twice daily.
- **Reference:** [ASIA Neurological Assessment](https://qte.csds.qld.edu.au/wp-content/uploads/sites/7/2024/07/neurological-assessment.pdf)
- **Ask:** Is the ASIA test being performed? How frequently? What is his current ASIA score? Can we see the results?

## MODERATE — Should Be Addressed

### 13. VTE prophylaxis unclear

- Polytrauma, immobilised, pelvic fractures — extremely high VTE risk.
- Bleeding risk from pelvic injury may contraindicate chemical prophylaxis, but this should be an active, documented decision.
- **Ask:** Is he on clexane, heparin, or TED stockings only? Is there a documented VTE risk assessment and prophylaxis plan?

### 14. No documented log roll / perianal exam

- With L1 crush, sacral fracture with displacement, and pelvic ring disruption — cauda equina / sacral nerve root injury is a real possibility.
- Perianal sensation and tone should be documented.
- **Ask:** Was a log roll performed? What was perianal sensation and tone? Any bladder/bowel dysfunction beyond what's attributable to catheterisation?

### 15. Bowel care not prescribed

- Opioids (fentanyl) + immobility + gabapentin = high constipation risk. No aperients visible on the drug chart.
- See also item #12 (bulbocavernosus reflex) — the outcome of that test dictates the full bowel protocol.
- Michael has a catheter in situ. In the absence of a Jordan frame, the usual Australian practice is not a diaper but blueys laid flat underneath him to minimise rolling.
- **Ask:** Are stool softeners / laxatives prescribed? When were bowels last open?

### 16. Pressure management

- In an ideal world a Jordan frame would be used to rotate Michael. In its absence, a log-roll style rotation with two nurses is the substitute — with extreme caution given spinal injuries.
- It is positive that he is on a rotating air mattress.
- Staff should be checking for red spots at bony prominences, especially the sacrum / top of the natal cleft ("top of the bum crack") — this is the danger zone.
- **Reference:** [Jordan Lifting Frame](https://www.safetyandmobility.com.au/shop/item/jordan-lifting-frame)
- **Ask:** Is a Jordan frame available? How frequently is he being turned? Are pressure areas being documented?

### 17. Open vs closed fracture status not confirmed for any injury

- If any fracture is open, prophylactic IV antibiotics and tetanus vaccination are time-critical.
- **Ask:** For each fracture site (elbow, wrist, pelvis) — confirmed closed? Any overlying lacerations or skin breaks?
